The 31st meeting of the Isaac and Celia Sklar Family Group was held at the home of Max and Ann on Sunday March 7th. Meeting was called to order by our Pres. William, who welcomed Mr. and Mrs. Kessler and Leon and Rose who were guests at this meeting.

The customery minute of silence was observed.

Minutes of previous meeting were read and corrections noted and finally approved on motion made by Ann.

A letter of thanks was received from newlyweds Buddy and Ruth for the gift presented to them from the Family Group.

Dan reported on the wedding of Bud and Ruth and expressed the sentiments of the Group on the ceremony and presenting to them of a gift of money from Group. Irving also was called on to report and he stated that the impressive ceremony and hospitality shown was enjoyed by all. We are also very proud to hear that Buddy has been accepted at Yale which is a great honor and our best wishes go to Bud and Ruth.

Louis gave a very fine report on the beautiful Briss for our newest addition to the Group, mainly Fredric Barton Willensky, which is the talk of the town. Those of us who attended certainly had a grand time. Best of luck to the proud parents and grandparents. May we have many many more such occassions.

Congratulations were also extended to William and Gussie on Bobby's convalescing, and we hope that he will return from Lakewood in good health.

Our Yor Zeit this year is on April 3rd. and we will go to the cemetary the Sunday preceeding or the Sunday following at which time we will also have a meeting when we return. Our Pres. and Gussie offered to take this meeting, which will be held in the Recreation Room.

Bills for gift and flowers were presented for payment and Irving moved said bills be paid, so carried. Dues were collected for 1946/1947 and the amount of \$46.00 was collected.

Harriet acted as chairman on the Happy Day on behalf of Dorothy who was unable to attend this meeting as her mother is staying at their home. A letter will be sent with our good wishes to Mrs. Botwinick. Harriet reported the sum of \$4.21 collected and a round of applause was extended to all who had happy occassions.
