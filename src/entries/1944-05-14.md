The 21st meeting of the I. & C. Sklar Family Group was held on Mother's Day, Sam and Mary hosts. Meeting was called to order by Pres. Max at 5:30 P.M.

Pres. Max greeted guests and hoped that they would enjoy being with us.

Customary minute of silence was observed.

Minutes of previous meeting was read and had correction to make — about family functions not included in minutes. Correction noted. Max spoke about Family Group Gathering in home of Louis and Sophie, in honor of Morty, Bobby and Leon Selfgeman and wife while the boys were home, as well as celebrating the silver anniversary of Louis and Sophie.

Louis thanked Group for gift from Group and was only sorry that Sam was not present at party, since he was ill. Sam answered that since he missed the 4th, 25th, he hoped to be with them on 50th anniversary.

Pres. Max also spoke about engagement party of Dan and Harriet, at which the group was invited and a gift presented.

A letter of thanks was received from Harriet and Dan for gift presented by Group.

Under old business the matter of the family pictures was finally straightened out and each family is to receive a picture and the children to receive one as a gift when they get married from the group. The amount paid for the pictures was \$28.50 which sum was reimbursed to William and Irving who paid for same. Each family paid \$3.50 towards same and \$7.00 balance was reimbursed from treasury.

Nat announced his daughter Harriet's coming wedding on June 25th at Temple Ohab Hassad at 1:30 P.M. promptly and invited the Family Group. Dan said he also enjoyed being with Group and hoped we would all come to his wedding and that Harriet would recover from being hoarse.

An announcement of a shower to be given to Ruth Gale was announced for Tuesday May 23rd and everyone was invited.

Dues were collected \$4.00.

Happy Day Fund: \$6.02.

Gift and flowers for Sophie and Louis 25th Anniv. was \$30.00.

Treasurer reported balance 5/14/44 \$41.03.

This meeting was the 5th anniversary of Group and Mama prayed that our children and family should stay well and prosper and increase in numbers. William also said a special prayer about our boys and prayed that the war be over and our boys come home safe and well.

Refreshments were served that were very delicious and greatly enjoyed by all.

The next meeting will be held Father's Day, and as usual we will go to the cemetery to pay our respects.

Hosts for the next meeting will be William and Gussie.

Respectfully submitted

B. Pinchuk, sec'y.
